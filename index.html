Paris is the capital and most populous city of France. With an estimated population of 2,165,423 residents as of 2019 in an area of more than 105 km¬≤ (41 sq mi), Paris is the fourth-largest city in the European Union and the 30th most densely populated city in the world in 2022.

Since the 17th century, Paris has been one of the world's major centres of finance, diplomacy, commerce, fashion, gastronomy, and arts and sciences. For its leading role in the arts and sciences, as well as its early and extensive system of street lighting, in the 19th century it became known as the City of Light.

History

The history of Paris dates back to approximately 259 BC, with the Parisii, a Celtic tribe settled on the banks of the Seine. In 52 BC, the fishermen village was conquered by the Romans, who called it Lutetia.

Medieval Paris

During the Middle Ages, Paris became one of Europe's major cities. The University of Paris, founded in the mid-12th century, was one of the first in Europe and attracted students from across the continent.

Geography

Paris is located in northern France, on both banks of the Seine River. The city is relatively flat, and the lowest point is 35 m (115 ft) above sea level. Paris has several prominent hills, the highest of which is Montmartre at 130 m (427 ft).

Culture

Paris is renowned for its museums and architectural landmarks. The Louvre received 2.8 million visitors in 2021, making it the most-visited art museum in the world. The city's Gothic Notre-Dame Cathedral and the Eiffel Tower are among the most visited monuments globally.
